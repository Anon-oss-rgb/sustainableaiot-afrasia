Overview

IoT sensor deployment is growing faster across Africa and South/Southeast Asia than in any other region, driven by low-cost hardware and mobile connectivity — yet the machine learning methods used to interpret this data are still largely designed and validated under grid-connected, high-bandwidth assumptions.

The consequences are direct: precision-agriculture systems that assume continuous connectivity fail during the drought or flood events they are meant to help manage; health-monitoring models trained on curated Global-North cohorts underperform on the populations that most need low-cost diagnostics; and cloud-dependent inference pipelines are unusable in off-grid or intermittently-powered settings. Researchers addressing these problems across the two continents are also frequently siloed by region, despite facing near-identical technical constraints in power, connectivity, and hardware heterogeneity.

This workshop treats African and Asian communities not only as deployment sites, but as sources of technical innovation, locally grounded knowledge, and new system-design principles — from disruption-tolerant field networks to frugal-hardware engineering practice — that can inform AIoT research globally. We adopt a broad, engineering-grounded notion of sustainability: beyond energy and carbon efficiency, this includes affordability, operational resilience, repairability, secure maintainability, and the local capacity to operate and improve AIoT systems over the long term.

Topics of Interest

Topics include, but are not limited to:

Efficient & edge ML

Compression, pruning, quantization, distillation, NAS for microcontrollers, TinyML, coreset selection, data-efficient training.

Sustainable & energy-aware learning

Carbon/energy-aware training and inference, solar/battery-constrained scheduling, hardware–software co-design for low-power sensing.

Distributed & on-device learning

Federated learning over heterogeneous, intermittently-connected devices; split computing; continual learning under drift.

Resilient networking, security & lifecycle management

Disruption-tolerant and intermittent networking, LPWAN and mesh networks, satellite/opportunistic connectivity, secure provisioning, firmware/model updates, long-term maintenance.

Sensing & perception for AIoT

Low-cost sensor fusion, robust perception under noisy hardware, remote-sensing/satellite-edge co-design.

Domain applications

Precision agriculture, inclusive livelihoods, accessible learning ecosystems, water and energy management, climate/disaster early warning, low-resource healthcare, smart mobility.

Human-centered & multilingual interfaces

Low-literacy interaction design, participatory data collection, community-in-the-loop deployment.

Data governance

Cross-border data sovereignty, privacy-preserving analytics, fairness and robustness under regional distribution shift.

SustainableAIoT-Afrasia 2026

Sustainable AIoT for AfroAsian Communities: Edge Intelligence for Resource-Constrained, Climate-Vulnerable Regions. A Workshop at the IEEE Annual Congress on Artificial Intelligence of Things (IEEE AIoT) 2026, 14–16 December 2026, Kobe, Japan.

SustainableAIoT-Afrasia invites original technical contributions on sustainable Artificial Intelligence of Things (AIoT) for resource-constrained, climate-vulnerable regions, with a particular focus on deployment contexts across Africa and Asia. Sustainability here is understood broadly — covering energy and carbon efficiency, affordability, operational resilience, repairability, secure maintainability, and local capacity to operate and evolve AIoT systems over time. We treat African and Asian communities not only as deployment sites, but as sources of technical innovation and locally grounded system-design knowledge.
